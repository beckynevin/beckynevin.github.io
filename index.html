UNDER CONSTRUCTION.

Currently, I am a graduate student at the University of Colorado Boulder in the Astrophysical and Planetary Science Department. I am also a member of the Mars Atmospheric Volatile EvolutioN (MAVEN) team. I use large global plasma models to simulate planetary magnetospheres and examine ion escape processes at Mars as well as terrestrial exoplanets. This has implications for long term atmospheric evolution and potential habitability. I typically use the global hybrid plasma code RHybrid and volumetric data analysis package yt, both of which I do some development work for.

I am also a Department of Energy Computational Science Graduate Fellow (CSGF) alumn and did a practicum at the National Renewal Energy Lab (NREL). This is a fantastic fellowship program that changed the course of my graduate career, I highly recommend that all first year grad students doing computational research apply.
